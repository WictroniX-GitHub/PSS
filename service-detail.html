Medium Voltage

MV systems operate between 1kV and 72kV and are crucial for efficient power distribution. They link substations to smaller transformers that then power industrial facilities, large commercial buildings, and even smaller substations.

Extra High Voltage

EHV systems are vital for long-distance transmission of high volumes of electricity, typically operating at 220kV or higher. They reliably transport power from major generation hubs to population and industrial centers.

Isolator

Isolators function as mechanical switches that create a safe physical break in electrical circuits, ensuring no current can flow during maintenance, repair, and upgrades. They are widely used in substations, power plants, and industrial settings.

Low Voltage

LT systems operate below 1kV and deliver power to homes, small businesses, and the everyday electrical outlets we use. They can also be utilized within industrial plants to power smaller machinery and control systems.

Retrofitting

Retrofitting offers compelling advantages over full system replacement. Retrofitting significantly reduces the capital expenses involved in power infrastructure projects, as it focuses on upgrading key components within the existing system. Because retrofits are adaptable to specific needs and can be performed in phases, they often cause less disruptions and operational downtime.
